When computing whether a taxpayer has realized a gain or loss on the sale, exchange or other disposition of an asset, a taxpayer must determine his or her basis in the asset. Basis is generally determined by the manner in which the taxpayer acquired the asset. Thus, if the taxpayer purchased the asset, basis is generally cost. After the taxpayer determines his or her basis in the asset, the next step is determining the taxpayer's "adjusted basis" at the time of disposition. Adjusted basis is cost or other basis increased by certain incurred costs such as capital improvements and decreased by certain deductions such as depreciation Specific basis adjustments are discussed in ¶1016(a)When Is a Basis Adjusted? and ¶1017§1017, Discharge of Indebtedness.
